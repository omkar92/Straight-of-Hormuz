# 🚢 Strait of Hormuz Maritime Risk Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes maritime traffic, geopolitical events, and oil prices to identify patterns affecting global trade and risk. The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world’s most critical oil transit routes, making it essential to understand how disruptions impact supply and pricing.

---

## 📊 Datasets Used
- Ship Traffic Data (vessel movement, speed, type)
- Oil Prices (Brent & WTI)
- Geopolitical Events (event type, severity)

---

## 🧠 Objectives
- Analyze ship traffic trends over time  
- Evaluate the impact of geopolitical events on oil prices  
- Identify disruption patterns in maritime routes  
- Determine correlation between traffic and oil prices  
- Build a risk classification model  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Techniques
- SQL (Joins, CTEs, Window Functions)
- Data Aggregation & Filtering
- Time Series Analysis
- Analytical Thinking

---

## 📈 Key Analysis Performed
- Daily ship traffic analysis  
- Event impact on oil prices  
- Day-over-day price change calculation  
- Rolling averages for trend analysis  
- Ranking and segmentation of traffic  
- Risk classification based on severity, traffic, and pricing  

---

## 🔥 Key Insights
- Event severity has a stronger impact on oil prices than traffic volume  
- High-risk scenarios occur when:
  - High severity events  
  - Low ship traffic  
  - Rising oil prices  
- No strong inverse correlation between traffic and oil prices was observed  
- Certain patterns indicate potential disruption in maritime trade  

---

## ⚠️ Business Impact
- Helps shipping companies avoid high-risk periods  
- Supports better decision-making in global trade logistics  
- Assists in monitoring geopolitical risk and price fluctuations
